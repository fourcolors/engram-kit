Built from the current tracker and result artifacts. This is the "what all those JCB steps were actually doing" version.

Date: 2026-04-21 Task tracker: JCB Scope: judge calibration, not architecture ranking

1) The one-paragraph story

JCB was a 63-task risk-burn-down program to make sure our judge instrument is trustworthy before we compare memory architectures. We split the work into construct definition, packet/schema hygiene, judge reliability, judge-bias measurement, rubric diagnostics, robustness checks, and "later methods" gates. The result is not "who won architecture." The result is: we measured what is stable, found what is confounded, and pinned what data is still missing.

In short: the checklist was a scientific control system, not a feature backlog.

5) What is proven vs not proven

Proven enough to use now

  • Judge identity materially shifts verdicts on same answers (+0.5 clean, +0.5694 in 4-rep clean extension).
  • Judge stochasticity is real and nontrivial; exact number depends on estimator and judge family.
  • The current 12-subcategory rubric is over-specified/collapsed around strongly correlated latent signal.
  • Abstention/coverage framing is practical on current subset (13 accepted, 17 abstained; audited false-premise capture 1.0 in subset).

Not proven yet (and explicitly blocked/deferred)

  • Architecture ranking claims: out of scope until sigma_beta and stronger human calibration are available.
  • Clean generation variance (JCB-030) is blocked by missing repeated fresh generations under fixed configs.
  • Correlated judge error on gold subset (JCB-021) is blocked by missing human-reviewed labels.
  • Later methods are intentionally gated: CyclicJudge rotation, conformal intervals, IRT, bias-bounded guarantees.

6) PM readout: what to tell stakeholders today

We ran a calibration program, not a model race. The 63-task checklist existed to make verdicts interpretable first.

The instrument has measurable behavior now. We can quantify judge disagreement, judge stochasticity, and rubric collapse with reproducible numbers from disk.

Two blockers remain before architecture claims. Human-labeled calibration subset and clean repeated generations per model/condition/probe.

This is good science progress. We now know exactly which claims are safe to make and which are still hypothesis-only.
