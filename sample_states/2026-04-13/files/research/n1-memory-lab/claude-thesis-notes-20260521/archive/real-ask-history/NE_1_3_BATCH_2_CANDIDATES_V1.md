# NE-1.3 Batch 2 Candidates V1

This is the second discovery batch of `5` real asks.

These widen the object beyond the core `6` without losing the real continuity
burden.

## Candidate 1

**Ask**

`what was I last working on in syke? what was left to do? what were the most recent threads and decisions?`

**Why include**

- strongest pre-burst baseline for work-state recovery
- moves beyond pure handoff/resume into unfinished-work truthfulness
- good bridge between “where are we now?” and later “what stayed live?”

## Candidate 2

**Ask**

`what projects am I working on?`

**Why include**

- expands from thread recovery to project-surface recovery
- tests whether memory collapses everything into one dominant repo/thread
- useful for evaluating whether the system preserves a broader working model

## Candidate 3

**Ask**

`what was the memory tools landscape study? what did we learn about memorix, claude-mem, and other tools? what patterns were worth adopting vs skipping? what is the cross-platform federation gap?`

**Why include**

- broadens from continuity-only into learning transfer
- tests whether durable lessons survive, not just activity recall
- strong for “what survived as usable understanding?”

## Candidate 4

**Ask**

`what have we learned from actual usage about how data flows across platforms?`

**Why include**

- one of the best true cross-harness asks
- reaches beyond thread map into platform interaction and evidence flow
- useful for the larger thesis that benchmarks miss the real object

## Candidate 5

**Ask**

`what was learned about making the observe layer robust and federated? what design decisions were made about adapters, the five signals, session handling, and avoiding compounding mistakes?`

**Why include**

- strong architecture-learning ask
- keeps corrections and design-memory in play
- good for testing whether the system can preserve decisions under pressure

## Batch 2 role

Batch 1 core `6` covers:

- currentness
- temporal reconstruction
- changed-state tracking
- handoff fidelity
- live-vs-residue
- exact-thread resumption

Batch 2 adds:

- unfinished-work recovery
- project-set recovery
- durable learning transfer
- cross-platform usage understanding
- architecture-learning under pressure

Together they form a much better month-scale base for the eventual `50`.
